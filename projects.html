Project One

Digital images represent two-dimensional scenes and can be easily manipulated using advanced image processing software like Adobe Photoshop and Corel Draw. This allows users, even novices, to add or remove features without obvious signs of tampering, making it challenging to assess the authenticity of images. Authenticity is crucial in fields like forensics, medical imaging, e-commerce, and the film industry. In medical contexts, diagnoses often rely on accurate imaging. This paper proposes a method for detecting image tampering using the copy-move technique, which involves copying and pasting parts of an image to create a fake version.
